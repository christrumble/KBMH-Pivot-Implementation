# Doc A — Account & Renewal Memo

**Audience:** Matt Hoag (KBM COO, merged-company COO)
**From:** Marcus Dallacqua (Proprio) & Chris Trumble (GSI)
**Date:** May 3, 2026
**Status:** Decision memo — confirms recommendation made by NetSuite leadership and aligned with merged-company financial posture

---

## Decision

**The merged company will operate inside the KBM Hoag NetSuite account. The Pivot Interiors NetSuite account will be wound down at the next renewal and will not be renewed.**

This memo documents the reasoning, confirms the path, and flags the operational steps that follow from the decision.

## Why this is the right call

The two accounts are not equivalent in their financial posture:

| | KBM Hoag | Pivot Interiors |
|---|---|---|
| Contract type | 5-year prepaid (NetSuite + Orion) | Annual subscription |
| Years remaining | ~5 (year 1) | Year 2 starting at next renewal |
| Renewal due | Not for ~5 years | Imminent (May 2026) |
| Sunk cost if abandoned | High (full term prepaid) | Low (continue paying nothing once renewal lapses) |

KBM's 5-year prepayment is a financial asset that does not transfer if the account is abandoned. Walking away from it to consolidate inside Pivot's account would mean writing off the prepaid value and continuing to pay annual fees indefinitely — the worst possible combination. Continuing inside KBM and letting Pivot's contract lapse at renewal preserves the asset and removes a recurring cost.

NetSuite leadership confirmed this same posture in our recent meeting: their preferred path is for the merged company to operate inside the prepaid KBM account. NetSuite will not consolidate the two accounts (they would lose revenue), but they are not opposed to one of them lapsing — and Pivot's annual structure makes the lapse mechanically simple.

## Action items

1. **Do not renew the Pivot Interiors NetSuite contract.** Allow it to lapse at the May 2026 renewal date. No counter-offer, no extension.
2. **Initiate Partner-of-Record transition** for the KBM account so that the merged company's GSI/Proprio relationship is correctly recorded against the surviving account. License-related decisions should wait until the POR transition completes.
3. **Plan Pivot data migration into the KBM account** as part of the Realize phase resuming in June. The unification recommendation (Doc B, in progress) governs how Pivot's configuration choices and data are absorbed into the surviving account.
4. **Communicate the decision** to Sandra Rudloff and Pivot leadership before the renewal lapse so the lapse is intentional, not surprise. The unification messaging is "we are operating inside KBM's account because the contract structure made that the right financial choice; the system will reflect both companies' best processes."

## Licensing posture

Per recent reconciliation, Pivot has approximately 200 employees in their NetSuite account today, against approximately 100 purchased licenses. Of those, only 10–15 employees actively use NetSuite; the rest are inactive employee records. **Do not request additional licenses until the Partner-of-Record transition is complete.** The merged company's true license requirement should be sized after consolidation against the KBM contract, and any expansion should be negotiated with NetSuite at that point — not pre-emptively against either legacy footprint.

## What this does not decide

- **How configurations merge** — that is the work of Doc B (Process Unification Recommendation) and the on-site working session that follows.
- **Operational timing of the Pivot system shutdown** — Pivot's account will remain accessible through the lapse and through whatever data extraction window is required for migration. Specific cutover dates are scheduled inside the Realize phase plan.
- **Branding or company-record naming** — separate decision; not blocking and not in scope here.

## Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Pivot users lose access before migration window closes | Schedule data extraction *before* the renewal lapse; if extraction takes longer than expected, negotiate a short paid extension on Pivot account only as a contingency |
| NetSuite changes posture during POR transition | POR transition initiated in writing; reference NS leadership's verbal alignment on consolidation path |
| Pivot stakeholders perceive the decision as KBM "winning" | Frame in unification communications as a financial/contract decision, not a process decision; Doc B will demonstrate that processes are merged on merit |
| License sizing miscalculation post-merge | License count is held flat through POR transition; resized only after merged organization is operating |

## Next steps

1. This memo is shared with Matt for confirmation.
2. Once confirmed, formal non-renewal notification is sent to NetSuite for the Pivot account.
3. Partner-of-Record transition request is submitted for the KBM account.
4. Sandra Rudloff and Pivot leadership are briefed before the renewal lapse becomes visible to their internal IT.
5. Doc B (Process Unification Recommendation) continues on its own track and is the basis for how the merged company actually operates inside the surviving account.
