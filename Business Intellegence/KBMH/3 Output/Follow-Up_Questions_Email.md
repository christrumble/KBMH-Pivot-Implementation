**Subject:** NetSuite Business Intelligence Implementation – Strategic Decisions Needed

Dear [Client Leadership],

Thank you for the comprehensive discovery sessions we've completed around Business Intelligence and Reporting. We're excited about the direction we're heading and the real-time reporting capabilities that will transform how your teams work together.

To move forward with implementation and ensure we build exactly what your organization needs, we have **five strategic areas** where we need your input and decisions. These aren't blockers—they're opportunities to shape the system to your specific needs.

### 1. **Export Security & Data Protection**

As we discussed, NetSuite provides role-based export controls to help protect sensitive data like customer lists and pricing. We need to establish your export governance:

- **Notification Threshold:** At what data volume should we trigger alerts? (We're thinking 50MB as a starting point—does that make sense for your business?)
- **Role-Based Export Access:** Which roles should have full export capability? Which roles should have limited or no export permissions?
- **Special Cases:** Are there any exceptions or special circumstances we should plan for?
- **Audit Trail:** What's your preference for audit trail retention—do regulatory requirements apply?

**Why this matters:** This protects your business while keeping your teams productive.

---

### 2. **Official Reports & Governance Standards**

One of the biggest wins we can deliver is ending the "Mark's version, Matt's version, Kip's version" problem through standardized reporting. We need clarity on:

- **Official Report Inventory:** Can each department (Finance, Sales, Operations, Order Management, Pre-Quote) provide a list of the 5-10 reports they actually rely on daily/monthly?
- **Authority & Updates:** Who has the authority to designate a report as "official"? What's the process for updating an official report?
- **Naming & Documentation:** Should we use a prefix (like "GSI-Official: Revenue Summary") to make official reports instantly recognizable?
- **Version Control:** How should we handle obsolete versions—archive or delete?

**Why this matters:** This becomes your single source of truth and eliminates reporting debates.

---

### 3. **Dashboard Publishing & Customization**

Your teams will have both personal dashboards (on the Home tab) and department-specific published dashboards. We need to decide:

- **Lock-Down Strategy:** Which roles should get "locked" dashboards they can't modify? (This ensures everyone in Finance, Sales, etc. is looking at the same metrics.)
- **Flexible Customization:** Which roles have the flexibility to personalize their views?
- **Override Process:** If someone needs an exception, what's the approval process?

**Why this matters:** Locked dashboards ensure alignment in meetings; flexible ones empower individual contributors.

---

### 4. **Training & Adoption**

We want to ensure consistent interpretation and rapid adoption. Key questions:

- **Interpretation Standards:** Beyond "here's the report," we need to document *what each metric means*. Who from your team can help us write these guides?
- **Training Timeline:** When should training happen relative to go-live? Weeks before, or right before?
- **Train-the-Trainer:** Should we train your power users who then train their teams, or direct training to all users?
- **Ongoing Support:** Post-go-live, who's your internal support person when questions arise?

**Why this matters:** Training determines whether this becomes a transformation or just a new system.

---

### 5. **Power BI & External Reporting Tools**

We want to understand your current environment and future direction:

- **Current Usage:** Are you currently using Power BI? If so, for which reports/dashboards?
- **Strategic Decision:** Should we retire Power BI and consolidate into NetSuite, or maintain it for specific use cases?
- **If Retained:** Do you need a live connection to NetSuite (real-time data) or is scheduled data OK?
- **Report Migration:** Which reports should we migrate into NetSuite, and which (if any) should stay in Power BI?
- **SharePoint & Google Drive:** What's currently stored in these platforms for reporting? Should we migrate into NetSuite?

**Why this matters:** Consolidation = single source of truth. Fragmentation = ongoing trust issues.
